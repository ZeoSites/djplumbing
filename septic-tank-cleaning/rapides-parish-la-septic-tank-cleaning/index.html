Types Of Septic Tank Systems Available

During the process of septic tank installation, experts take multiple factors into thought, including which include the size of the structure, local weather conditions, climatic conditions of the location, nature of the soil, and more.

All of these aspects influence in determining the appropriate variety of septic tank system to be. Below are numerous varieties of septic tank systems ready for installation at DJ Plumbing Septic Tank Services:

Conventional Tank System

Commonly described as a gravity-based sewage system, this kind of septic system is both the most prevalent and the most straightforward to install and maintain. This septic tank system doesn't demand any extra complex electrical or technological elements. Instead, it banks solely on natural forces, specifically gravity.

Chamber Septic Tank System

The chamber septic tank system, features chambered drains usually crafted from plastic, specifically high-density polyethylene. Nevertheless, it's worth noting that other materials can also be used for these chambers.

Over the past three decades, this particular type of septic tank system has gained considerable recognition, primarily due to its economical installation and ease of installation. Caring for it is akin to a similar process to the conventional a standard septic tank system.

Mound Septic Tank System

This septic tank system is utilized for properties situated on shallow soil or in close proximity to surface bedrock. This type of tank system requires forming a constructed mound consisting of distinct levels of soil, gravel, and sand as the drainfield.

In contrast to standard septic tank system, this type of septic tank system utilizes electrical energy, as well as requires periodic maintenance and examinations, unlike the standard septic tank system, which is substantially more straightforward to manage.

Aerobic Treatment Unit

Referred to as Aerobic Treatment Units (ATUs), this specific septic tank system utilizes an electric pump to facilitate oxygen into the septic tank. The presence of oxygen enables oxygen-loving microorganisms to flourish within the septic tank. This procedure causes the decomposition of waste materials contained within the septic tank.

Usually, this type of sewage tank is constructed in locations near surface water or where there are hostile soil characteristics. It should be emphasized that maintaining this type of septic tank system can be quite expensive, as it requires continuous maintenance treatments.

Recirculating Sand Filter Septic Tank System

This septic tank system utilizes sand filtration to process and redistribute of wastewater. To function effectively, this system requires the use of electricity. When compared to various septic tanks, the setup and ongoing care of such a system can be relatively expensive.

Tips For Homeowners on Septic System Health in Rapides Parish LA.

As we are all aware, no residence is complete without a septic tank. For those of us who own houses, it is imperative to show great care to keeping up of your sewage system's condition, since it directly affects not only your health but also the environment.

Imagine the inconvenience of waking up early in the morning and realizing that your toilet is backed up, or putting up with the noxious stench emanating from a sewage tank spill.

Below are some simple steps for homeowners to adhere to in order to properly care for the condition of their septic system.

Regular Inspection and Pumping

Usually, a home sewage tank should be professionally inspected on a regular basis, typically every 3-5 years by experienced technicians. Throughout this inspection, the expert inspectors can check for seepage, inspect the top and base of your waste tank, evaluate the accumulated scum and sludge in your sewage tank, and execute skilled pumping of your waste tank.

Efficient Use Of Water

The lower the amount of water in a home equates to reduced water entering the septic tank. Restroom flushing habits accounts for about 25-30% of water consumption in the residential property. The most effective method to reduce water utilization in the toilet involves substituting current models with high-efficiency ones, which use fewer gallons of water for each flushing.

High Efficiency Shower Heads

The water utilized during showering is also sent to the septic tank. To minimize this, a prudent step is to utilize high-efficiency shower appliances, specifically designed to lower water consumption.

Proper Disposal of Waste

Many people possess the unfortunate practice of dealing with the bathroom as if it were a waste receptacle. However, practicing this conduct ultimately results in septic system obstructions. Therefore, homeowners must ensure that all occupants in the home disposes of waste properly and avoids it in the toilet.

Seasonal Septic System Care Advice

Just as looking after the septic system each year, it is equally vital to administer seasonal care and maintenance to the sewage tank system. Below are several tips for seasonal care and maintenance across the four seasons:

Fall (Autumn)

Ensuring proper maintenance of the septic system plays a significant role in preparing for the harsh winter weather.

  • It is advisable the sewage tank during the fall, as it's hard in the winter months.
  • In the fall, limit the use of lawn mowers within the vicinity of the sewage tank, while do not use automobiles in this area by any means.
  • Additionally, make sure to protect the treatment soil from cold temperatures.

Winter

Taking care of septic systems during the winter might be substantial hurdles, given the freezing temperatures. Hence, it's wise to start preparing for cold weather ahead of time, typically in autumn.

The most important care you can offer your septic tank system in winter involves regularly inspecting the commonly affected zones where septic systems might freeze, with the goal of avoiding this problem. These locations are:

  • The conduit emanating from the tank in your residence
  • The piping that goes to the soil treatment zone
  • The designated soil treatment zone
  • And the septic tank itself
Spring
  • Taking care of your septic system during spring involves the following steps:
  • When the sun is out and the snow is thawing, it's a suitable opportunity to schedule a expert inspection of your septic tank. This assessment ensures the health of your septic tank.
  • Springtime provides a great moment to replace your filter. The filter may be obstructed during the winter months, potentially causing malfunctions in the septic system.
  • Spring is an opportune moment to schedule a septic tank pumping. This helps maintain the efficiency of your septic tank.
Summer

Proper septic system maintenance for the summer season involves the following steps:

  • Warm weather in the summer can intensify the odor from your septic tank, so property owners should be prepared to contact professional septic tank technicians in the summer months.
  • The use of water tends to go up in the summer, resulting in more water entering the septic tank. To minimize this, switch to high-efficiency toilets that consume less water.
