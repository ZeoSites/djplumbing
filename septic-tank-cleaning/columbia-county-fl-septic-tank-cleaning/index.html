Types Of Septic Tank Systems Available

When septic tank installation, experts take multiple factors into account, including which include the size of the structure, local weather conditions, environmental factors of the area, nature of the soil, and so forth.

These elements play a role in choosing the right type of septic tank system to be. Below are numerous varieties of sewage tank systems available for installation at DJ Plumbing Septic Tank Services:

Conventional Tank System

Also known as a gravity-fed septic system, this kind of septic system is both the most prevalent and the simplest to set up and keep in good condition. This particular septic tank system doesn't need any extra complex electrical or technological elements. Instead, it banks solely on natural forces, specifically gravity.

Chamber Septic Tank System

Commonly known as the pressure distribution septic system, features chambered drains typically constructed from high-density polyethylene plastic. Nevertheless, it's worth noting that other materials can be utilized for these chambers.

During the previous 30-year period, this particular type of septic tank system has gained considerable recognition, primarily due to its economical installation and straightforward setup. Caring for it is akin to a parallel pattern to that of a standard septic tank system.

Mound Septic Tank System

This septic tank system is utilized for buildings positioned on shallow soil or in close proximity to surface bedrock. This type of tank system involves forming a constructed mound composed of distinct levels of soil, gravel, and sand as the drainfield.

In contrast to the traditional septic tank system, this particular septic tank system uses electrical energy, and it also requires regular maintenance and inspections, in contrast to the standard septic tank system, which is considerably more straightforward to manage.

Aerobic Treatment Unit

Referred to as ATU or Aerobic Treatment Unit, this specific septic tank system makes use of an electric pump to facilitate oxygen into the septic tank. The presence of oxygen enables oxygen-loving microorganisms to prosper within the septic tank. This procedure results in the biodegradation of waste materials contained within the septic tank.

Typically, such septic tank is constructed in locations adjacent to surface aquatic environments or in areas with hostile soil characteristics. It's important to note that caring for such septic tank system comes with a high cost, as it requires ongoing maintenance treatments.

Recirculating Sand Filter Septic Tank System

This septic tank system makes use of sand filtration to process and redistribute of wastewater. To work efficiently, this system requires the use of electricity. When compared to other types of sewage tanks, the installation and maintenance of this system tends to be relatively expensive.

Tips For Homeowners on Septic System Health in Columbia County FL.

As we are all aware, every dwelling is complete without a septic tank. For those of us who own houses, it's vital to show great care to keeping up of your sewage system's well-being, as it has a direct impact not only your own well-being but also the environment.

Visualize the inconvenience of waking up early in the morning only to find that your toilet is clogged, or having to endure the noxious stench emanating from a sewage tank spill.

The following are some simple steps for householders to comply with in order to properly care for the wellness of their sewage system.

Regular Inspection and Pumping

Usually, a residential septic tank needs to be professionally examined at least every 3-5 years by experienced technicians. During this inspection, the professional examiners will examine for seepage, inspect both the upper and lower sections of your tank, evaluate the accumulated scum and sludge in your sewage tank, and execute professional maintenance of your tank.

Efficient Use Of Water

The lower the amount of water in a home equates to less water entering the sewage system. Restroom usage accounts for about 25-30% of water consumption in the household. An optimal approach to decrease water usage in the restroom is by replacing older models with low-flow ones, which use fewer gallons of water for each flushing.

High Efficiency Shower Heads

The water utilized during showering is also directed into the sewage system. To minimize this, the smart choice is to utilize low-flow shower appliances, specifically designed to lower water consumption.

Proper Disposal of Waste

Numerous folks possess the unfortunate practice of treating the bathroom as if it were a waste receptacle. Nonetheless, engaging in this conduct ultimately results in sewage system obstructions. So, householders must ensure that everyone in the household disposes of waste appropriately and avoids it down the toilet.

Seasonal Septic System Care Advice

As crucial as looking after the sewage system annually, it is equally vital to administer season-specific care and maintenance to the sewage tank system. Below are several tips for seasonal care and maintenance across the four seasons:

Fall (Autumn)

Ensuring proper maintenance of the sewage system plays a significant role in preparing for the harsh winter weather.

  • Consider pumping the septic tank during the fall, as doing so becomes challenging in the winter months.
  • During the fall season, limit the use of grass cutters in the area near the septic tank, and avoid using vehicles in this area by any means.
  • Moreover, take measures to shield the treatment area from freezing.

Winter

Caring for septic systems during the winter can pose significant challenges, given the freezing temperatures. That's why, it's wise to anticipate the winter season in advance, preferably in the fall.

The crucial care you can offer your sewage system during winter involves regularly inspecting the four common areas where septic systems are prone to freezing, aiming to prevent this problem. These areas include:

  • The pipeline leading from the tank in your residence
  • The tube leading to the soil treatment zone
  • The section for soil treatment
  • In addition to the septic tank itself
Spring
  • Proper septic system care for the spring season involves the following steps:
  • When the sun is out and the snow is thawing, it's the perfect time to schedule a expert inspection of your septic tank. This assessment ensures the health of your septic tank.
  • Springtime provides a great moment to replace your filter. The filter might have become clogged during the winter months, potentially causing septic system issues.
  • Spring also presents plan a septic tank pumping. This helps ensure optimal performance of your septic tank.
Summer

Summer septic system care involves the following steps:

  • The summer heat can intensify the septic tank odors, so homeowners should be prepared to seek professional septic tank services during the summer months.
  • The use of water tends to go up during the summer, causing more water going into your septic tank. To mitigate this, switch to high-efficiency toilets that are more water-efficient.
