Types Of Septic Tank Systems Available

During the process of installing a septic tank, skilled technicians take multiple factors into account, including which include the size of the structure, local weather conditions, climatic conditions of the location, nature of the soil, and more.

All of these aspects influence in choosing the appropriate type of septic tank system to be. Below are numerous varieties of sewage tank systems ready for installation at DJ Plumbing Septic Tank Services:

Conventional Tank System

Commonly described as a gravity-fed septic system, this specific type of septic system is the most widely used and the simplest to set up and keep in good condition. This septic tank system doesn't demand any further complex electrical or technological elements. In contrast, it banks solely on natural forces, specifically gravity.

Chamber Septic Tank System

The chamber septic tank system, features drain chambers usually crafted from plastic, specifically high-density polyethylene. Nevertheless, it's worth noting that other materials can also be used for these chambers.

During the previous three decades, this particular type of septic tank system has gained considerable recognition, primarily due to its cost-effectiveness and straightforward setup. Caring for it follows a parallel pattern to the conventional a standard septic tank system.

Mound Septic Tank System

This particular septic tank system is utilized for properties situated on thin soil or in close proximity to surface bedrock. This type of tank system requires forming a man-made mound consisting of distinct layers of soil, gravel, and sand as the drainfield.

As opposed to the traditional septic tank system, this type of septic tank system uses electrical energy, and it also demands periodic maintenance and examinations, unlike the standard septic tank system, which is substantially easier to maintain.

Aerobic Treatment Unit

Also known as ATU or Aerobic Treatment Unit, this type of septic tank system makes use of an electric pump to facilitate oxygen into the septic tank. The addition of oxygen enables aerobic microbes to flourish within the septic tank. This process causes the biodegradation of the waste contained within the septic tank.

Usually, this type of sewage tank is usually built in locations near surface water or in areas with unfavorable soil conditions. It's important to note that caring for this type of septic tank system comes with a high cost, because it demands ongoing maintenance treatments.

Recirculating Sand Filter Septic Tank System

This particular septic tank system makes use of sand filtration for the treatment and recirculation of wastewater. To function effectively, this system needs the use of electricity. As opposed to other types of septic tanks, the installation and maintenance of this system tends to be on the pricier side.

Tips For Homeowners on Septic System Health in Miami County KS.

As everyone knows, no residence is complete without a waste tank. As responsible homeowners, it is imperative to pay meticulous attention to the proper maintenance of your sewage system's condition, as it has a direct impact not only your health but also the surrounding environment.

Imagine the inconvenience of waking up early in the morning only to find that your toilet is clogged, or putting up with the noxious stench wafting from a sewage tank spill.

Below are a couple of straightforward procedures for householders to adhere to to maintain maintain the wellness of their septic system.

Regular Inspection and Pumping

Typically, a residential sewage tank needs to be professionally examined on a regular basis, typically every 3-5 years by skilled professionals. Throughout this inspection, the professional examiners can examine for leaks, check the top and base of your waste tank, analyze the accumulated scum and sludge in your tank, and execute skilled maintenance of your waste tank.

Efficient Use Of Water

Minimizing water usage in a residence equates to reduced water entering the septic tank. Toilet flushing habits accounts for approximately 25% to 30% of water use within the residential property. An optimal approach to reduce water usage in the toilet involves substituting older models with low-flow fixtures, employing less gallons of water per flushing.

High Efficiency Shower Heads

The water utilized during showering is also sent to the septic tank. To decrease this, the smart choice involves using low-flow shower appliances, crafted to decrease water use.

Proper Disposal of Waste

Many people exhibit the bad habit of dealing with the bathroom as if it were a waste receptacle. However, practicing this behavior ultimately results in septic system obstructions. So, householders must ensure that everyone in the household disposes of waste appropriately and doesn't flush it in the toilet.

Seasonal Septic System Care Advice

As crucial as maintaining the sewage system each year, it is equally vital to administer season-specific maintenance to the septic tanks system. Below are a few tips for year-round maintenance across the four seasons:

Fall (Autumn)

Ensuring proper maintenance of the sewage system is crucial in preparing for the harsh winter weather.

  • Consider pumping the sewage tank before winter arrives, as it's challenging when winter sets in.
  • During the fall season, restrict the use of grass cutters in the area near the sewage tank, while avoid using automobiles in this area by any means.
  • Moreover, take measures to protect the treatment area from cold temperatures.

Winter

Taking care of septic systems during the winter might be substantial hurdles, given the freezing temperatures. Hence, it's advisable to start preparing for cold weather in advance, preferably in the fall.

The crucial care you can offer your sewage system in winter includes regularly inspecting the four common areas where septic systems might freeze, aiming to prevent this problem. These areas include:

  • The conduit emanating from the house's tank
  • The piping leading to the area designated for soil treatment
  • The designated soil treatment zone
  • And the waste tank
Spring
  • Proper septic system care for the spring season involves the following steps:
  • When the sun is out and the snow is thawing, it's a suitable opportunity to schedule a professional septic inspection. This inspection ensures the health of your septic tank.
  • Springtime provides a great moment to replace your septic tank filter. The filter may be obstructed during the winter months, potentially causing malfunctions in the septic system.
  • Spring also presents schedule a tank maintenance. This helps ensure optimal performance of your septic tank.
Summer

Summer septic system care involves the following steps:

  • The summer heat can heighten the odor from your septic tank, so property owners ought to be ready to seek professional septic tank services in the summer months.
  • The use of water tends to increase in the summer, resulting in more water entering the septic tank. To mitigate this, switch to high-efficiency toilets that are more water-efficient.
