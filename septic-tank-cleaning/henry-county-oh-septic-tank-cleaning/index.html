Types Of Septic Tank Systems Available

During the process of septic tank installation, skilled technicians consider various elements into thought, including which include the size of the structure, weather patterns, climatic conditions of the location, nature of the soil, and so forth.

All of these aspects play a role in determining the appropriate variety of septic tank system to be. Below are the various types of septic tank systems ready for installation at DJ Plumbing Septic Tank Services:

Conventional Tank System

Commonly described as a gravity-based sewage system, this kind of septic system is both the most prevalent and the simplest to set up and keep in good condition. This particular septic tank system doesn't need any further complex electrical or technological elements. Instead, it banks solely on the force of gravity.

Chamber Septic Tank System

Commonly known as the pressure distribution septic system, utilizes chambered drains usually crafted from plastic, specifically high-density polyethylene. Nevertheless, it's important to point out that other materials can be utilized for these chambers.

During the previous 30-year period, this particular type of septic tank system has acquired significant popularity, primarily due to its cost-effectiveness and ease of installation. Caring for it follows a similar process to the conventional a standard septic tank system.

Mound Septic Tank System

This particular septic tank system is utilized for properties situated on thin soil or in close proximity to surface rock formations. This type of tank system involves creating a man-made mound consisting of distinct layers of soil, gravel, and sand as the drainfield.

In contrast to the traditional septic tank system, this type of septic tank system utilizes electrical energy, as well as demands regular maintenance and inspections, in contrast to the typical septic tank system, which happens to be considerably easier to maintain.

Aerobic Treatment Unit

Referred to as ATU or Aerobic Treatment Unit, this specific septic tank system utilizes an electric pump to facilitate oxygen into the septic tank. The addition of oxygen allows aerobic microbes to flourish within the septic tank. This procedure results in the decomposition of waste materials in the septic tank.

Typically, this type of sewage tank is usually built in locations near surface aquatic environments or where there are unfavorable soil conditions. It's important to note that caring for this type of septic tank system comes with a high cost, because it demands continuous maintenance treatments.

Recirculating Sand Filter Septic Tank System

This particular septic tank system utilizes sand filtration for the treatment and recirculation of wastewater. To work efficiently, this system requires the use of electricity. As opposed to various sewage tanks, the setup and ongoing care of such a system can be on the pricier side.

Tips For Homeowners on Septic System Health in Henry County OH.

As we are all aware, every dwelling is complete without a septic tank. For those of us who own houses, it is imperative to show great care to the proper maintenance of your septic system's condition, since it directly affects not only your own well-being but also the surrounding environment.

Visualize the inconvenience of rising early and realizing that your toilet is clogged, or putting up with the noxious stench wafting from a sewage tank spill.

The following are a couple of straightforward steps for householders to adhere to in order to properly care for the condition of their sewage system.

Regular Inspection and Pumping

Typically, a home sewage tank needs to be professionally inspected on a regular basis, typically every 3-5 years by skilled professionals. During this process, the expert inspectors will check for leaks, inspect both the upper and lower sections of your tank, analyze the accumulated scum and sludge in your sewage tank, and perform skilled pumping of your tank.

Efficient Use Of Water

Minimizing water usage in a residence results in reduced water going into the sewage system. Toilet usage makes up approximately 25% to 30% of water use in the household. The most effective method to decrease water usage in the toilet is by substituting current fixtures with high-efficiency fixtures, which use fewer gallons of water per flushing.

High Efficiency Shower Heads

Water used during bathing is also sent to the sewage system. To decrease this, the smart choice is to utilize high-efficiency shower heads, specifically designed to decrease water use.

Proper Disposal of Waste

Many people exhibit the unfortunate practice of treating the toilet as if it were a waste receptacle. Nonetheless, practicing this behavior ultimately results in sewage system clogs. Therefore, householders must ensure that everyone in the household gets rid of refuse properly and avoids it down the toilet.

Seasonal Septic System Care Advice

As crucial as looking after the septic system each year, seasonal care is just as important to provide season-specific care and maintenance to the septic tanks system. Listed are several recommendations for seasonal care and maintenance for the four seasons:

Fall (Autumn)

Ensuring proper maintenance of the septic system plays a significant role in anticipating for severe winter weather.

  • Consider pumping the sewage tank during the fall, as it's challenging when winter sets in.
  • In the fall, restrict the use of grass cutters within the vicinity of the sewage tank, while avoid using vehicles in this area by any means.
  • Additionally, make sure to protect the treatment soil from freezing.

Winter

Taking care of septic systems during the winter might be substantial hurdles, given the freezing temperatures. That's why, it's wise to start preparing for cold weather ahead of time, preferably in the fall.

The most important attention you can offer your septic tank system in winter involves periodically examining the four common areas where septic systems are prone to freezing, with the goal of avoiding this problem. These locations are:

  • The conduit emanating from the house's tank
  • The tube leading to the soil treatment zone
  • The designated soil treatment zone
  • And the septic tank itself
Spring
  • Proper septic system care for the spring season involves the following steps:
  • As the sun begins to shine and the snow starts melting, it's the perfect time to plan a professional septic inspection. This assessment ensures the health of your septic tank.
  • Springtime offers an excellent opportunity to replace your filter. The filter may be obstructed during the winter months, potentially causing malfunctions in the septic system.
  • Spring also presents plan a tank maintenance. This helps maintain the efficiency of your septic tank.
Summer

Summer septic system care involves the following steps:

  • Warm weather in the summer can intensify the odor from your septic tank, so homeowners should be prepared to contact professional septic tank technicians in the summer months.
  • Water usage tends to increase in the summer, causing more water entering the septic tank. To minimize this, switch to high-efficiency toilets that consume less water.
