Types Of Septic Tank Systems Available

During the process of septic tank installation, skilled technicians consider various elements into thought, including but not limited to the scale of the property, local weather conditions, environmental factors of the area, soil type, and more.

These elements play a role in choosing the appropriate variety of septic tank system for installation. Below are the various types of septic tank systems ready for installation at DJ Plumbing Septic Tank Services:

Conventional Tank System

Also known as a gravity-fed septic system, this specific type of septic system is the most widely used and the most straightforward to install and maintain. This particular septic tank system doesn't need any further electrical or technological work. Instead, it relies exclusively on natural forces, specifically gravity.

Chamber Septic Tank System

Commonly known as the pressure distribution septic system, features drain chambers usually crafted from plastic, specifically high-density polyethylene. Nevertheless, it's important to point out that different materials can be utilized for these chambers.

During the previous 30-year period, this particular type of septic tank system has gained considerable recognition, primarily due to its cost-effectiveness and straightforward setup. Caring for it is akin to a parallel process to that of a regular sewage tank system.

Mound Septic Tank System

This septic tank system is employed for buildings situated on shallow soil or in close proximity to surface bedrock. The installation of this tank system requires creating a constructed mound consisting of distinct levels of soil, gravel, and sand as the drainfield.

As opposed to the traditional septic tank system, this particular septic tank system utilizes electrical energy, and it also demands regular maintenance and inspections, unlike the typical septic tank system, which happens to be considerably easier to manage.

Aerobic Treatment Unit

Also known as ATU or Aerobic Treatment Unit, this specific septic tank system utilizes an electric pump to facilitate oxygen into the septic tank. The addition of oxygen enables oxygen-loving microorganisms to prosper within the septic tank. This process results in the decomposition of waste materials contained within the septic tank.

Typically, such sewage tank is usually built in locations adjacent to surface aquatic environments or where there are unfavorable soil characteristics. It should be emphasized that maintaining such septic tank system comes with a high cost, because it demands continuous maintenance treatments.

Recirculating Sand Filter Septic Tank System

This septic tank system utilizes a layer of sand fill for the treatment and recirculation of wastewater. To function effectively, this system needs the installation of electricity. When compared to other types of septic tanks, the installation and maintenance of this system can be on the pricier side.

Tips For Homeowners on Septic System Health in Chatham County GA.

As we are all aware, no residence is complete without a waste tank. For those of us who own houses, it is imperative to pay meticulous attention to the proper maintenance of your sewage system's well-being, as it has a direct impact not only your own well-being but also the environment.

Visualize the inconvenience of rising early only to find that your toilet is clogged, or putting up with the unpleasant odor emanating from a septic tank leak.

The following are a couple of straightforward steps for householders to comply with in order to maintain the condition of their septic system.

Regular Inspection and Pumping

Typically, a home sewage tank needs to be professionally examined at least every 3 to 5 years by skilled professionals. During this inspection, the professional examiners will check for seepage, check both the upper and lower sections of your tank, analyze the accumulated scum and sludge in your sewage tank, and execute professional maintenance of your tank.

Efficient Use Of Water

Minimizing water usage in a home results in less water entering the septic tank. Restroom flushing habits makes up approximately 25% to 30% of water consumption within the residential property. The most effective method to reduce water usage in the toilet is by substituting current models with low-flow fixtures, employing less gallons of water per flush.

High Efficiency Shower Heads

The water utilized during showering is also directed into the septic tank. To minimize this, the smart choice involves using low-flow shower heads, crafted to decrease water consumption.

Proper Disposal of Waste

Numerous folks exhibit the bad habit of dealing with the toilet as if it were a trash can. Nonetheless, engaging in this behavior inevitably septic system obstructions. So, householders should make sure that everyone in the household gets rid of refuse appropriately and doesn't flush it down the toilet.

Seasonal Septic System Care Advice

As crucial as looking after the sewage system annually, seasonal care is just as important to provide season-specific maintenance to the sewage tank system. Below are a few tips for year-round maintenance across the seasonal changes:

Fall (Autumn)

Taking proper care of the sewage system is crucial in anticipating for the harsh cold winter weather.

  • It is advisable the sewage tank during the fall, as it's hard in the winter months.
  • In the fall, restrict the use of grass cutters in the area near the sewage tank, while do not use vehicles in this area at all costs.
  • Moreover, make sure to protect the treatment soil from freezing.

  • The conduit emanating from the house's tank
  • The tube that goes to the area designated for soil treatment
  • The section for soil treatment
