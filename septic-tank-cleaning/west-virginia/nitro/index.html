Tips For Homeowners on Septic System Health in Nitro, WV.

As we are all aware, every dwelling is complete without a waste tank. For those of us who own houses, it is imperative to pay meticulous attention to the proper maintenance of your septic system's condition, as it has a direct impact not only your own well-being but also the environment.

Imagine the hassle of waking up early in the morning only to find that your toilet is backed up, or putting up with the noxious stench emanating from a septic tank leak.

Below are a couple of straightforward steps for homeowners to comply with to maintain properly care for the condition of their sewage system.

Regular Inspection and Pumping

Typically, a home sewage tank needs to be professionally examined at least every 3-5 years by experienced technicians. Throughout this process, the professional examiners can check for seepage, check the top and base of your tank, analyze the accumulated scum and sludge in your sewage tank, and perform skilled maintenance of your tank.

Efficient Use Of Water

The lower the amount of water in a home equates to less water entering the sewage system. Restroom usage makes up approximately 25% to 30% of water consumption in the residential property. An optimal approach to decrease water usage in the restroom involves substituting older models with high-efficiency ones, which use fewer gallons of water for each flushing.

High Efficiency Shower Heads

The water utilized during bathing is also sent to the sewage system. To minimize this, a prudent step involves using high-efficiency shower appliances, specifically designed to lower water consumption.

Proper Disposal of Waste

Many people exhibit the unfortunate habit of treating the bathroom as if it were a waste receptacle. However, engaging in this behavior inevitably sewage system clogs. So, homeowners must ensure that all occupants in the home gets rid of waste properly and avoids it in the toilet.

Types Of Septic Tank Systems Available

During the process of septic tank installation, experts take multiple factors into thought, including which include the size of the property, weather patterns, climatic conditions of the location, nature of the soil, and more.

These elements influence in determining the right variety of septic tank system for installation. Here are numerous varieties of sewage tank systems available for installation at DJ Plumbing Septic Tank Services:

Conventional Tank System

Commonly described as a gravity-based sewage system, this kind of septic system is both the most prevalent and the simplest to set up and keep in good condition. This particular septic tank system doesn't need any extra electrical or technological work. In contrast, it relies exclusively on the force of gravity.

Chamber Septic Tank System

The chamber septic tank system, features chambered drains usually crafted from plastic, specifically high-density polyethylene. Nevertheless, it's important to point out that different materials can be utilized for these chambers.

During the previous three decades, this variety of septic tank system has gained significant popularity, primarily due to its cost-effectiveness and ease of installation. Caring for it is akin to a similar process to the conventional a standard septic tank system.

Mound Septic Tank System

This particular septic tank system is employed for properties situated on shallow soil or adjacent to surface rock formations. The installation of this septic system requires forming a man-made mound consisting of distinct layers of soil, gravel, and sand as the drainfield.

As opposed to the traditional septic tank system, this type of septic tank system utilizes electrical energy, and it also demands periodic maintenance and examinations, in contrast to the standard septic tank system, which is considerably easier to maintain.

Aerobic Treatment Unit

Referred to as ATU or Aerobic Treatment Unit, this type of septic tank system utilizes an electric pump to facilitate oxygen into the septic tank. The addition of oxygen allows oxygen-loving microorganisms to prosper within the septic tank. This process causes the biodegradation of the waste contained within the septic tank.

Usually, such sewage tank is constructed in regions adjacent to surface water or in areas with unfavorable soil characteristics. It's important to note that caring for such septic tank system comes with a high cost, as it demands continuous maintenance treatments.

Recirculating Sand Filter Septic Tank System

This particular septic tank system utilizes sand filtration to process and redistribute of wastewater. To function effectively, this system requires the use of electricity. As opposed to other types of septic tanks, the setup and ongoing care of such a system tends to be on the pricier side.
