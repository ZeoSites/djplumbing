Types Of Septic Tank Systems Available

When installing a septic tank, experts take multiple factors into account, including but not limited to the size of the property, weather patterns, climatic conditions of the location, soil type, and so forth.

These elements play a role in determining the appropriate type of septic tank system to be. Here are the various types of sewage tank systems ready for installation at DJ Plumbing Septic Tank Services:

Conventional Tank System

Commonly described as a gravity-based sewage system, this kind of septic system is the most widely used and the most straightforward to install and maintain. This septic tank system doesn't demand any extra complex electrical or technological elements. In contrast, it banks solely on the force of gravity.

Chamber Septic Tank System

Commonly known as the pressure distribution septic system, utilizes drain chambers usually crafted from plastic, specifically high-density polyethylene. Nevertheless, it's important to point out that different materials can also be used for these chambers.

During the previous 30-year period, this variety of septic tank system has gained significant popularity, primarily due to its economical installation and straightforward setup. Caring for it is akin to a similar pattern to that of a standard septic tank system.

Mound Septic Tank System

This particular septic tank system is employed for buildings positioned on shallow soil or adjacent to surface bedrock. This type of septic system involves forming a constructed mound consisting of distinct layers of soil, gravel, and sand as the drainfield.

In contrast to standard septic tank system, this particular septic tank system uses electrical energy, as well as requires periodic maintenance and examinations, in contrast to the standard septic tank system, which is considerably more straightforward to manage.

Aerobic Treatment Unit

Also known as Aerobic Treatment Units (ATUs), this type of septic tank system utilizes an electric pump to facilitate oxygen into the septic tank. The presence of oxygen enables aerobic microbes to flourish within the septic tank. This procedure results in the biodegradation of the waste in the septic tank.

Typically, such septic tank is constructed in regions adjacent to surface aquatic environments or in areas with hostile soil conditions. It should be emphasized that caring for such septic tank system comes with a high cost, as it requires ongoing maintenance treatments.

Recirculating Sand Filter Septic Tank System

This particular septic tank system utilizes a layer of sand fill to process and redistribute of wastewater. To work efficiently, this system needs the installation of electricity. As opposed to various septic tanks, the installation and maintenance of such a system can be relatively expensive.

Tips For Homeowners on Septic System Health in Stevens County MN.

As everyone knows, no residence is complete without a septic tank. As responsible homeowners, it is imperative to show great care to keeping up of your sewage system's condition, as it has a direct impact not only your health but also the environment.

Visualize the inconvenience of waking up early in the morning and realizing that your toilet is clogged, or putting up with the noxious stench emanating from a septic tank leak.

The following are some simple procedures for homeowners to comply with to maintain maintain the wellness of their septic system.

Regular Inspection and Pumping

Usually, a residential septic tank needs to be professionally examined at least every 3 to 5 years by skilled professionals. During this process, the professional examiners can check for leaks, inspect the top and base of your tank, analyze the accumulated scum and sludge in your sewage tank, and perform skilled maintenance of your tank.

Efficient Use Of Water

Minimizing water usage in a residence results in less water entering the sewage system. Toilet flushing habits accounts for about 25% to 30% of water use within the residential property. An optimal method to reduce water usage in the toilet is by replacing current fixtures with high-efficiency fixtures, which use less gallons of water per flush.

High Efficiency Shower Heads

Water used when bathing additionally sent to the sewage system. To minimize this, the smart choice is to utilize high-efficiency shower heads, specifically designed to decrease water use.

Proper Disposal of Waste

Numerous folks possess the bad habit of dealing with the toilet as if it were a waste receptacle. Nonetheless, practicing this conduct inevitably septic system obstructions. So, homeowners must ensure that all occupants in the home gets rid of waste appropriately and avoids it in the toilet.

Seasonal Septic System Care Advice

As crucial as looking after the septic system annually, it is equally vital to administer season-specific care and maintenance to the septic tanks system. Listed are several recommendations for year-round care and maintenance across the four seasons:

Fall (Autumn)

Taking proper care of the septic system is crucial in preparing for the harsh cold winter weather.

  • Consider pumping the septic tank during the fall, as it's challenging when winter sets in.
  • During the fall season, limit the use of grass cutters within the vicinity of the septic tank, while avoid using vehicles in this area at all costs.
  • Additionally, take measures to protect the treatment soil from freezing.

Winter

Taking care of septic systems throughout the winter can pose significant challenges, given the freezing temperatures. Hence, it's wise to anticipate cold weather ahead of time, preferably in the fall.

The most important care you can provide for your sewage system in winter includes periodically examining the four common areas where septic systems might freeze, aiming to prevent this problem. These areas include:

  • The pipeline leading from the house's tank
  • The piping that goes to the area designated for soil treatment
  • The designated soil treatment zone
  • In addition to the waste tank
Spring
  • Taking care of your septic system during spring involves the following steps:
  • As the sun begins to shine and the snow starts melting, it's a suitable opportunity to schedule a professional septic inspection. This inspection ensures the health of your septic tank.
  • Springtime provides a great moment to change your septic tank filter. The filter might have become clogged during the winter months, potentially causing septic system issues.
  • Spring is an opportune moment to schedule a tank maintenance. This helps ensure optimal performance of your septic tank.
Summer

Summer septic system care involves the following steps:

  • The summer heat can heighten the septic tank odors, so property owners ought to be ready to seek professional septic tank services in the summer months.
  • The use of water tends to go up in the summer, resulting in more water entering the septic tank. To minimize this, consider using high-efficiency toilets that consume less water.
